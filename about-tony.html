Why I built this

About Tony

Welcome!

Hi, I'm Tony Ferlazzo.

If you're a new driver—or someone who's never felt completely comfortable owning a car—you're exactly the reason I created this website.

Confidence comes from understanding.

For many years I worked in Information Technology, helping people solve problems with computers and software. What I enjoyed most was taking complicated subjects and explaining them in ways that made sense.

After retiring, I began thinking about another subject that intimidates many people: cars. Not because they're impossibly complicated, but because most people were never taught the basics.

Why I Created the Driver Confidence Guide

Many drivers know how to drive but have never been shown how to check the oil, understand dashboard warning lights, check tire pressure, safely pump gas, find the owner's manual, or know when to ask for help.

That isn't their fault. Nobody ever taught them.

Adventure Learning uses episodes, choices, recurring characters, and practical skills to make that learning more enjoyable.

My Goal

I'm not trying to turn anyone into a mechanic. I want drivers to become confident car owners.

If this series prevents an expensive mistake, avoids a breakdown, or gives someone the confidence to open the hood for the first time, it has accomplished what I hoped.

This Guide Is Just the Beginning

When I started creating the Driver Confidence Guide, I thought I was writing about cars. I eventually realized I was doing something more important: preserving lessons I had learned so they could help someone else.

That realization inspired a bigger idea. Every person has experiences worth sharing—a retired teacher, a nurse, a mechanic, a parent, a veteran, a gardener, or a business owner.

Each has learned lessons that could make someone else's journey a little easier.

The Driver Confidence Guide is the first example of that idea. My long-term goal is to help ordinary people turn their own life experiences into Learning Adventures that educate, encourage, and inspire others.

If this guide helped you, perhaps you have something worth teaching, too.

How AI Helped

I used modern artificial intelligence as a creative partner to organize ideas, improve explanations, develop software, and turn a simple vision into the website you're visiting today.

To me, AI doesn't replace creativity. It amplifies it.
