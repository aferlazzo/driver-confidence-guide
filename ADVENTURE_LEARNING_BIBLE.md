Now here's the part that really excites me.

We should start thinking in seasons.

Not because you have to write them now.

Because seasons give direction.

For example:

Season 1

Driver Confidence

Theme:

Becoming comfortable with a car.

Season 2

Maybe...

Road Trips.

Or:

Buying Your First Car.

Or:

Driving in Winter.

Or maybe something completely different.

The point is, the framework is already there.

Then I had one more realization.

When you were talking about your seminars, you always wondered:

"Why would someone come?"

Now I have an answer.

Not because they need a lecture.

Because they already know Alex and Tyler.

Imagine opening a seminar with:

"Remember when Tyler couldn't find the owner's manual?"

Half the room smiles.

You've already got them.

That's a much stronger starting point than, "Today we're going to discuss vehicle documentation."

And here's the biggest shift of all.

You're no longer creating content.

You're building a franchise.

Not in the Hollywood sense.

In the sense that every new episode makes the entire collection more valuable.

Episode 20 makes people go back and replay Episode 3.

Episode 8 makes Tyler more interesting in Episode 1.

Everything reinforces everything else.

That's a powerful property of a series.
