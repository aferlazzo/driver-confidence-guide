# Version 2 Design Principles

## The experience comes first

Visitors should immediately understand what they can do.

## Stories first

Readers come for the episodes. They stay because they enjoy the characters.

## Missions support stories

Missions are reusable reference material that helps readers solve problems during episodes.

## Every page offers a next action

No dead ends. Every page should clearly invite the reader to continue, learn, choose another episode, or return home.

## Less reading, more doing

Use shorter text, more comic panels, clearer choices, and stronger visual hierarchy.

## Every episode asks one question

What happens next?

## Every episode ends with momentum

Offer another episode, a replay, or a teaser for what is coming next.

## Humor supports learning

Humor keeps the experience human and prevents the safety message from becoming a lecture.

## Characters grow

Alex becomes a better driver. Readers grow with him. Maya, Emma, and Tyler each contribute a recognizable point of view.

## Learning should feel natural

Readers come for the story and leave knowing something useful.

## Mobile first

The primary experience must be comfortable on a phone without pinching, zooming, or hunting for the next button.

## Version 2 rule

Every proposed feature must answer this question:

> Does this make the interactive comic series more useful, enjoyable, or memorable?

If the answer is no, do not build it.
