Quick Guide

Find Your Owner's Manual

Every car is a little different. The owner's manual explains controls, warning lights, fuel requirements, maintenance schedules, and features that may be unfamiliar.

Alex's Situation

Alex has borrowed his mom's car and sees a dashboard button he does not recognize. Tyler suggests pressing it. Alex decides to find the owner's manual first.

How to Find It

📁

Check the glove compartment

Many cars still include a printed manual, often inside a zippered pouch with warranty and maintenance booklets.

🌐

Visit the manufacturer's website

Search using the car's year, make, and model. Most manufacturers provide the official manual as a free PDF.

🔢

Use the VIN

The Vehicle Identification Number identifies the exact car. Look through the lower driver's-side windshield or on the driver's door jamb.

📱

Check the dashboard screen

Many newer vehicles include a searchable digital owner's manual in the infotainment system.

Finding the Digital Manual

When the printed manual is missing, the manufacturer's website is usually the fastest backup. The VIN can help you find the exact manual for that vehicle.

Look in the Car, Too

Many newer vehicles include a digital manual directly in the dashboard system. Search for “Owner's Manual,” “Vehicle Information,” or “Help.”

Try It Yourself

Quick Tip

Download the PDF while you have Wi-Fi. Then it will be available even when you are away from home.

Tyler Says...

“I thought every car had the same buttons.”

What Alex Learned

Every car really is different. Two minutes spent locating the manual can prevent a lot of guessing.
